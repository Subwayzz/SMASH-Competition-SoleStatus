Information About Diabetes

Learn about diabetes, its types, symptoms, and management.




What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is when an individual's glucose (blood sugar levels) are higher than normal. There are two main types of diabetes: Type 1 and Type 2. Type 1 is when the body can not properly create insulin at all while Type 2 is when the body can not create enough, or the insulin created in their body does not work. Common Forms of Testing for Diabetes

  • A1c tests (blood test measuring average blood sugar levels)
  • Greater than 6.5 = Diabetes
  • 5.7 to 6.4 = Prediabetic
  • Less than 5.7 = normal


Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Thirst and hunger
  • Infections
  • Urinating often
  • Blurred vision


Causes

  • Genetic mutations
  • Insulin resistance
  • Pancreatic damage


Long-term effects

  • Heart disease
  • Hearing damage
  • Kidney damage


Solutions

  • Cut out additional sugars from diet
  • Limit processed foods
  • Eat a balanced diet and healthy food
  • Regular exercise


Fun Facts

  • 1.2 million Americans are diagnosed with diabetes every year.
  • Diabetes is not necessarily caused solely by extreme sugar intake.
  • Diabetes can be prevented with lifestyle changes.
  • 13% of the population has diabetes.
